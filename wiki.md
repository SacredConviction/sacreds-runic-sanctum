# Runes and Rules

Runes are magical sigils that can be inscribed onto equipment to enhance their capabilities. Runes, if they are not already inscribed into items, generally come in the form of a small, metal chisel imbued with magic called Rune Chisels. Rune Chisels are usually three to five inches in length and half to three-quarters of an inch thick. Rune Chisels are usually inscribed in Draconic, Giant, Elvish, or Primordial, although Rune Chisels can be found and carved in all languages.

A talented individual can inscribe a Rune by touching it to a compatible object and instill the magic from the chisel into the item. The chisel then becomes a mundane piece of metal. Likewise, to remove a Rune a metal chisel is touched to the object and the magic is instilled back into the chisel. Adventurers often keep a small bag of chisels to retrieve and exchange Runes. A Rune instilled into a chisel or a piece of equipment can be discerned by an individual who speaks the Rune’s language, or by making a successful DC 18 Arcana check.

There are two types of Runes, Fundamental Runes and Property Runes. Only two Fundamental Runes can be inscribed on an object, of which one has to be a Potency Rune. A number of property runes can be added to an item equal to its potency rune bonus, 1 for +1, 2 for +2 and so on. Fundamental runes do not count against the limit of property runes that can be added.

To inscribe a Rune one must spend a long rest, or 8 hours of downtime, and pass a DC 14, 18, 22, 26, or 30 Arcana, Smith's Tool's, or Tinker's Tools check based on the Rune's Rarity(Uncommon-Artifact). On a success the Rune is inscribed,  if the check is failed by less than 10 the Rune inscribing fails, if the check is failed by 10 or more the Rune is destroyed. Only Runes that are of the same or lower rarity than the Potency Rune on the item can be inscribed. For example, A rare Property Rune cannot be inscribed on an item with only an uncommon Potency Rune. 

To remove a rune from an item one must spend a long rest and pass a DC 14, 18, 22, 26, or 30 Arcana, Smith's Tool's, or Tinker's Tools check based on the Rune's rarity(Uncommon-Artifact), on a success the rune is salvaged and on a failure it is not removed. Failing the check by 10 or more results in the destruction of the Rune.

Generally Runes can only be inscribed onto the equipment they are categorized under, for example, armor cannot be inscribed with Potent weapon or spell focus Rune. Some magic items, or class features may cause certain equipment, such as weapons to be able to be inscribed with a different category of Rune, such as a sword being used as a spellcasting focus for a Bladesinger. In such a case only one category of Potency Rune can be inscribed, for a weapon being used as a spell casting focus it can either have a weapon Potency Rune or a spellcasting focus Potency Rune but not both. Armor runes can be inscribed onto shields and normal clothing, such as robes, as well, functioning as normal. Inscribed clothing cannot sit underneath armor as Runes can be damaged by everyday movement.

Already magical items can still be inscribed with Runes, a +1 set of magical armor is considered a Potent set of armor. A mundane item inscribed with a Rune is considered to be of the same rarity of at least its highest rarity Rune. Already magic items are also considered to be of the same rarity of at least their highest rarity Rune. More specialized magic items such as a Robe of the Archmagi or a Holy Avenger are considered to be inscribed with runes of equivalent value to their bonus’, contributing toward Fundamental and Property Rune limits. For example, a Robe of the Archmagi is considered to be inscribed with a Potent(Superior) Spell Focus Fundamental Rune, because of this it cannot be inscribed with a Potent armor, or weapon Fundamental Rune but can be inscribed with a Focusing Fundamental Rune.
