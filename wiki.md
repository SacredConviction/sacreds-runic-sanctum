Only one Potency and one of the items other Fundamental Runes can be inscribed on a given object and to do so one must spend a long rest and pass a DC 14, 18, 22, 26, or 30 Arcana, Smith's Tool's, or Tinker's Tools check based on the Rune's Rarity. On a success the rune is successfully installed, if the check is failed by 10 or more the rune is destroyed. Weapon Potency runes increase the to hit and damage of a weapon by 1. A striking rune adds 1 weapon damage dice. For example, a great sword with a base damage of 2d6 would be increased to 3d6 and a great axe with a base damage of 1d12 would be increased to 2d12.
  
To remove a rune from armor or weapons one must spend a long rest and pass a DC 14, 18, 22, 26, or 30 Arcana, Smith's Tool's, or Tinker's Tools check based on the Rune's rarity, on a success the rune is salvaged and on a failure it is not removed. Failing the check by 10 or more results in the destruction of the Rune. 
 
A number of property runes can be added to an item equal to its potency rune bonus, 1 for +1, 2 for +2 and so on. Striking, Resilient, and Focused fundamental runes do not count against the limit of property runes that can be added.

Only Runes that are of the same or lower rarity than the Potency Rune on the item can be inscribed. For example, A rare Property Rune cannot be inscribed on an item with only an uncommon Potency Rune.

Armor runes can be inscribed onto normal clothing and shields as well, functioning as normal.
